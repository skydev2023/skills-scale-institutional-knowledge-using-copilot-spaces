# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers are responsible for user experience research, design prototypes, and facilitation of design reviews. They ensure that features meet usability standards before development and release.

### Responsibilities
- Explore user needs through research and usability testing
- Design wireframes, user flows, and interactive prototypes
- Review features for usability and accessibility before release
- Maintain and evolve the design system and component library
- Facilitate design critiques and stakeholder reviews

### Goals
- Ensure products are intuitive, accessible, and user-centered
- Reduce rework by catching usability issues early
- Align design decisions with product goals and user needs

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Developers and Product Managers
- Usability test reports and research summaries
- Prototype walkthroughs and annotation handoffs

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
The QA Lead oversees test planning, coordinates manual and automated testing efforts, and drives continuous quality feedback loops across the delivery team.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain the overall test strategy and test plans
- Coordinate test execution (manual and automated) across sprints and releases
- Lead defect triage and risk-based test prioritization
- Define and uphold the Definition of Done from a quality perspective
- Collaborate with Developers on test coverage and with Technical Writers on test documentation

### Goals
- Achieve and maintain agreed quality and test coverage thresholds
- Detect issues early to reduce cost and risk of late defects
- Enable confident, reliable releases

### Typical Communication
- QA status reports and defect triage sessions
- Test plans and test result summaries
- Release readiness sign-off with Project Managers

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers manage CI/CD pipelines, deployment automation, and monitoring/observability infrastructure to streamline and secure the software delivery process.

### Responsibilities
- Design, build, and maintain CI/CD pipelines and build automation
- Manage infrastructure-as-code, environments, and deployment configurations
- Set up and maintain monitoring, alerting, and observability tooling
- Coordinate deployment schedules and production rollouts with Project Managers
- Provide test and staging environments to support QA and development

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe, and repeatable deployments
- Maximize system reliability, uptime, and observability
- Reduce manual operational toil through automation

### Typical Communication
- Deployment runbooks and incident post-mortems
- Pipeline and infrastructure change notifications
- On-call and incident response coordination

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers maintain and improve internal and external documentation, ensuring clarity and accessibility for onboarding, support, and ongoing knowledge transfer across all team members.

### Responsibilities
- Author and maintain process docs, user guides, API references, and onboarding materials
- Collaborate with all team members to capture decisions, workflows, and best practices
- Review and edit documentation for clarity, consistency, and accuracy
- Manage the documentation lifecycle — creation, review, versioning, and deprecation
- Act as a bridge between technical contributors and non-technical stakeholders

### Goals
- Ensure all team members and stakeholders have access to accurate, up-to-date documentation
- Reduce onboarding time and single-person knowledge dependency
- Improve discoverability and usability of internal knowledge

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews with subject matter experts
- Change logs and documentation release notes
- Feedback loops with onboarding new team members
